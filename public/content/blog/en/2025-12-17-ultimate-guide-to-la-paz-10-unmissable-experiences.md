**La Paz, Bolivia's administrative capital, welcomes you at 3,640 meters above sea level** with dizzying energy. Between imposing mountains and an intense blue sky, this city is a labyrinth of contrasts where the ancestral and the modern intertwine. If you're planning your visit, this guide takes you through **the essential experiences** that make La Paz an unforgettable destination.

## 1. Fly Over the City on the Urban Cable Car

It's not just a means of transport; it's **the best introduction to La Paz**. With over 30 kilometers of cable, this cable car system is the most extensive and highest in the world.

* **Recommended lines**: The Red Line (from Central Station to El Alto) and the Yellow Line (spectacular panoramic views).
* **Approximate cost**: 3 BOB per segment (less than $0.50 USD).
* **Pro tip**: Take the cable car at sunset to see the city light up as the sky turns orange and pink.

## 2. Immerse Yourself in the Mystical Witches' Market

In the heart of the Rosario neighborhood, you'll find one of the most fascinating markets in the world. Here, **yatiris** (Aymara priestesses) offer everything from medicinal herbs to controversial **llama fetuses** for good fortune rituals.

* **What to find**: Amulets, potions, herbs, natural stones, and ritual objects.
* **Ideal time**: Monday to Saturday mornings.
* **Cultural respect**: Ask permission before photographing and avoid aggressive bargaining.

## 3. Stroll Down JaÃ©n Street, a Journey to the Colonial Past

This perfectly preserved cobblestone street houses **four important museums** in just two blocks. The colorful colonial facades will transport you to the 18th century.

* **Must-see museums**: Museum of Precious Metals, Litoral Museum, Casa Murillo Museum, and Museum of Musical Instruments.
* **Night activity**: On Fridays, the street transforms with craft fairs and live music.
* **Photography**: Best light in the afternoon when the sun illuminates the colorful facades.

## 4. Marvel at the Valley of the Moon

Just 10 kilometers from the center, this surreal landscape of eroded clay formations seems to transport you to another planet. The name is no coincidence: **the landscape truly evokes the lunar surface**.

* **Recommended schedule**: Arrive one hour before sunset.
* **Visit duration**: 1-2 hours maximum.
* **Accessibility**: Well-marked paths but with some stairs.

## 5. Explore the Black Market, a Universe of Shopping

Don't let the name confuse you: it's **the largest and most diverse market in La Paz**. Here you'll find absolutely everything, from car parts to handcrafted textiles.

* **Highlighted sections**: Handicrafts, electronics, traditional clothing, and food.
* **Shopping advice**: Prices are lower than in tourist areas.
* **Authentic experience**: Try fresh juices and empanadas from street stalls.

## 6. Visit the Killi Killi Viewpoint for the Best View

For a **free panoramic view** of the entire city, go up to the Killi Killi Viewpoint. From here, you can appreciate the urban expansion in the valley, surrounded by snow-capped mountains.

* **Best time**: At sunrise or sunset.
* **How to get there**: Taxi from downtown (5 minutes) or 15-20 minute uphill walk.
* **Safety**: Well guarded during the day, avoid visiting at night.

## 7. Discover Street Art: La Paz Graffiti

Street art in La Paz isn't vandalism; it's **recognized cultural expression**. The "La Paz Graffiti" project has transformed walls into canvases that tell social and political stories.

* **Key locations**: Sopocachi area, around Abaroa Square.
* **Recurring theme**: Social struggle, indigenous identity, and political criticism.
