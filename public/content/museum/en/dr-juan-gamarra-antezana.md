Passionate by his profession, he was a remarkable surgeon from La Paz, he defined the modern era of surgery in Bolivia.
The possibility to study his specialization in the United States of America, opened him the door to be a distinguished professional in La Paz.

"In Bolivia, cardiac surgery was started by Dr. Juan Gamarra Antezana on the 5th of March of 1953, the first ductus arteriosus ligation was performed by him. Two years later on the 25th of March of 1955, he also performed the first digital commissurotomy in a patient with mitral valve stenosis caused by rheumatic fever". Bolivian Medical History Society"
Dr. Juan Gamarra Antezana was professor at the "Facultad de Medicina" at Universidad Mayor de San Andres, where he taught dozens of Medicine students.
_Courtesy of the Bolivian Society for the History of Medicine_

During his professional live he performed hundreds of successful surgical interventions at State public hospitals like Cossmil and as a private practice as well.

Many distinguished personalities where operated by his hands, like Juan Lechin Oquendo (Union leader and political influencer of its time). But he also operated people with limited economic resources and all of them received the best treatment.

The possibility to study surgery in the United States, with wordly renamed surgeons such as Dr. Lahey and Dr. Catell among others, gave Juan a special appreciation for this country.
