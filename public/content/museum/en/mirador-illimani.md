## Illimani Viewpoint: Your Instagram Moment in La Paz

The **Illimani snowy peak**, iconic symbol of **La Paz**, is located in the **Cordillera Real** of the Bolivian Andes. With a height of **6460 meters above sea level**, it is Bolivia's second highest mountain and its Aymara name means "golden eagle". This imposing summit has been a source of inspiration for visual artists and musicians throughout Bolivian history.

### The Panoramic View

From this **privileged viewpoint** you get one of the **most complete views of La Paz**:

- **Illimani Snowy Peak**: The snow giant dominating La Paz horizon
- **Libertad Bridge**: One of the famous "triple bridges" of the city
- **Muela del Diablo**: Characteristic rock formation of La Paz landscape
- **Olympic Pool**: Located in **Alto Obrajes** neighborhood, South Zone
- **Iconic neighborhoods**: San Antonio Villa and Copacabana on eastern hillside
- **El Calvario Viewpoint**: Traditional observation point
- **Blue cable car**: Transportation system connecting to downtown
- **Libertador Station**: Hub of blue, green and yellow lines

### A Heritage in Transformation

**Climate change** visibly affects Illimani, which has lost snow mass in recent decades. At sunset, the **golden light** of the sun creates a unique visual spectacle reflecting on its slopes, offering **exceptional photographic moments** for residents and visitors.
