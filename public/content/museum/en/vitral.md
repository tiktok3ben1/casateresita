## Stained Glass Window: Illimani and Libertad Bridge

Original stained glass artwork inspired by the **rear view of La Casa de Teresita**. This unique piece captures two iconic symbols of La Paz through the traditional technique of stained glass.

### Artistic Creation

- **Designer**: Ernesto Gamarra F.
- **Master Glassmaker**: Manuel Segales (execution)
- **Inspiration**: Rear perspective of the house
- **Technique**: Traditional stained glass with lead came

### Symbolic Elements

The stained glass represents:

1. **Illimani Mountain**: The emblematic snowy peak of La Paz, visible from the house
2. **Libertad Bridge**: One of the famous "Triple Bridges" connecting:
   - San Jorge neighborhood (Arce Avenue)
   - Miraflores neighborhood (Los Leones Avenue)

### Artistic and Cultural Value

This stained glass window holds significance in the collection as it:

- **Translates architecture into art**: Converts a functional house view into artistic expression
- **Preserves traditional craft**: Showcases the mastery of Bolivian glassmaking
- **Creates light dialogues**: Transforms natural light into colored narratives
- **Connects interior and exterior**: Bridges the museum space with La Paz cityscape

### Technical Mastery

Executed by master glassmaker **Manuel Segales**, this work demonstrates:

- Precision in glass cutting and assembly
- Understanding of light and color properties
- Integration of contemporary design with traditional technique
- Architectural adaptation to the house structure

### Value in La Casa de Teresita Collection

This stained glass enriches the museum experience by:

1. **Adding a luminous dimension**: Introduces light as artistic medium
2. **Showing craft continuity**: Links traditional techniques with contemporary expression
3. **Creating visual connections**: Relates interior art with exterior urban landscape
4. **Documenting local mastery**: Highlights Bolivian artists in specialized crafts

As part of the contemporary section, this work demonstrates how **traditional techniques** can reinterpret **contemporary views**, creating a dialogue between craft heritage and modern artistic vision in Bolivia's cultural panorama.
